Executive Assessment

What is The Executive Assessment?

The Executive Assessment was introduced in march 2016 as a replacement for the GMAT. According to the GMAC, which administers The Executive Assessment, it was specifically designed for busy, mid-to-late career professionals

Who Accepts it ?

Executive Assessment is currently accepted at several leading business schools from around the world Such as

During the Executive assessment, you will be evaluated on the higher-order reasoning in three sections:
Integrated Reasoning,
Verbal Reasoning
Quantitative Reasoning.
The scoring scale for each section of the assessment is 0 to 20.
The total Score ranges from 100 to 200 All three sections are equally weighted in determining your total score.


Important points to note about the Executive Assessment.

While we broadly agree with the reasons given above for taking the executive assessment And believe that the executive assessment is a GREAT alternative for students who do not have the time to prepare for the GRE or the GMAT, there are some important points that students taking executive assessment ( even the GMAC if they are listening )need to be aware of:

1

The level of preparation

While the executive assessment claims that it only requires modest preparation, our view is different, A significant amount of time and effort goes into familiarizing yourself with the different question types and figuring out how to solve these questions in the time that is provided.

2

Number of Retakes

You can (at present) take the executive assessment only Twice in a lifetime, as opposed to 5 times in a year for Gmat (with a lifetime limit of 8 attempts). This is a significant problem since we have you known students who take the exam going by the modest preparation advice on the website and are left with only one attempt to get the score that the program asks for.

3

The score range

Theoretically, The scoring given out by the executive assessment should range between 100 and 200, but practically all the students we have seen have a total score that confirms to the formula 120 plus the scores received on the three subsections i.e. if you receive a quant score of 7, an integrated reasoning score of 10 and a verbal score of 12 your total score will be 120+7+10+12 = 149. So the score range of 100 and 200, and the practical score range of 120 to 180 are confusing, to say the least.

4

Lack of percentiles

one of the problems that students taking executive assessment need to grapple with is that (as on date) there are no percentiles given along with the executive assessment score, making it difficult both for the schools and the students to compare themselves and evaluate their chances.

5

Lack of official practice material

While the GMAC has said that the executive assessment will test concepts similar to the GMAT, it has not published any official material or practice tests, making it really difficult for students to have an estimate of how they will score on an official test

6

Breaks during exam

There are no brakes during the 90 minute duration of the executive assessment, that can cause significant fatigue as you solve the sections, we have had students who came to house after the first attempt and told us the main challenge they faced during the exam was to give the concentration levels during the Math section which happens to be the last section.

7

The exam order

Measurement does not give you choice to select the section you want to answer first, this has been a constant complaint with people taking the GMAT and at least while designing a new exam this feedback should be kept in mind and the student should have been allowed to answer the section they are most comfortable first.

8

Number of Schools

While the number of schools accepting executive assessment as a measure replacing the GMAT has gone up since the executive assessment was launched in 2016, there are still a number programs that might require you to take the GMAC. So before you decide to take the executive assessment please be sure that you have evaluated all the executive programs that you could go to and have narrowed down on the programs that require the executive assessment.

The 10 reasons the GMAC has given for taking the EXECUTIVE ASSESSMENT.

  1. The Executive Assessment was specifically designed to evaluate the business school readiness of seasoned professionals just like you.
  2. Because it was designed for busy professionals, the assessment includes just three short 30-minute sections with a total of 40 questions.
  3. The assessment focuses on the skills that are critical both to work and in an EMBA program: higher order reasoning, critical thinking, analysis, and problem-solving.
  4. The assessment requires a modest level of preparation. A lot of students would beg to differ, so do we. The executive assessment requires a fair degree of preparation.
  5. The assessment will help you (or your program) identify the skills you most need to sharpen in order to be successful in an EMBA program.
  6. You can reschedule your assessment as many times as you’d like, with no additional fees (as long as it’s at least 24 hours before your appointment).
  7. Taking the assessment is a convenient alternative to meet your application requirements.
  8. GMAC worked with top business schools to custom-build the assessment.
  9. The assessment provides an objective confirmation of the caliber of students in your cohort.
  10. You can take the assessment year-round at over 600 conveniently located test centers.

Saves a lot of Time

Most senior executives are continually on the move around the world,making them miss out on classes and leaving them with discontinuities in preparation. A lot of students stay nowhere close to places where the classes are held and the others who do stay close still have to spend a significant amount of time making it to the class, time that can be more effectively utilized preparing.
