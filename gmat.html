WHAT IS THE GMAT ?

Created and developed by the Graduate Management Admissions Council (GMAC), the GMAT is a standardized test designed as an entry requirement for business school programs.

The test isconsidered to be one of the most accurate and objective indicators of potential success in business administration, finance, and management programs.

THE GMAT EXAM

The exam has four sections:

  • Analytical Writing Assessment (AWA) Section

    This section is designed to measure your ability to write clearly, think critically and form cohesive arguments. You’ll be given 30 minutes to craft an essay responding to an argument-based writing prompt; your essay should critically evaluate the content of the prompt and offer suggestions for improving the logic of the argument. GMAT will grade the essay based on the quality of your critical analysis, your counterargument(s) and your ability to write.

  • Integrated reasoning

    The Integrated Reasoning section measures your ability to evaluate and interpret information and data using graphs, data sets, and other formats. The section consists of 12 different questions, and you’ll be given 30 minutes to complete all of them. Questions are delivered in a multiple-choice, ‘yes or no’ format or require you to fill in data tables by clicking boxes with your mouse. You are allowed to use a calculator in this section

  • Quantitative reasoning

    Quantitative reasoning (not MATH !!!!!! very important difference that many test takers fail to get) GMAT uses core concepts of arithmetic, algebra, and geometry to test your quantitative ability and higher order reasoning. In the Quantitative section of the GMAT, you will have 75 minutes to complete 37 questions. You are not allowed to use a calculator in this section. Questions in the Quantitative section can be classified into two categories.

    you’ll be given a mathematical question accompanied by some data, and then asked to determine whether or not you’ve been given enough information to answer the question.

    delivered in standard multiple-choice format, you’ll be given a mathematical expression or a question, and then asked to select the best of five possible answers.


  • Verbal reasoning

    The Verbal reasoning (not ENGLISH!!!!!!!!!!!)GMAT exam tests your reading, grammar, and Analytical skills. The section consists of 41 questions, in multiple-choice format, and you’ll have 75 minutes to complete all of them. The Verbal section is divided into three core areas.
    Reading comprehension:
    You’ll be given several reading prompts; each passage is accompanied by at least one question asking you to identify the main idea, tone or other details about the reading.
    Critical reasoning:
    For each question, you’ll be given an argument to understand and a task. You have to choose the best of five possible answers.
    Sentence correction:
    Read sentences and correct them for grammar, word choice, sentence structure, and other qualities.

SCORING ON THE GMAT.

The scoring on the GMAT is a little complex and you need to spend time to understand this.
Official GMAT score card will contain 5 different components
1 Analytical writing assessment (AWA)
The analytical writing assessment on the GMAT is scored on a scale of 0 to 6.
2 Integrated reasoning section (IR)
Integrated reasoning section is scored on a scale of 1 to 8.
3 Quantitative reasoning
The quantitative reasoning section is scored on a theoretical scale of 0-60 but practically the scores range from 10 to 51. A score of 51 is the 99th percentile.
4 Verbal reasoning
The verbal reasoning section is scored on a theoretical scale of 0 to 60 but practically the scores range from 9 to 46,. A score of 46 is the 99th percentile
It's important to note that the scores on the quantitative and verbal sections are not the same as the number of questions you got correct.
So ignore the raw scores and focus on the percentiles, that makes the understanding the section easier.
5 The total score on the exam.
The score is calculated by combining your quantitative and verbal percentile and ranges between 200 and 800. The AWA and IR scores are not considered while calculating the overall score.

The quantitative and verbal sections of the GMAT use a computer algorithm based on the principle of adaptive testing. Each section starts off with questions of average difficulty and based on the responses given by the student the exam makes the questions either more difficult or easy.The exam repeats this process till the end of the section to get an estimate of the student's competence levels.


The GMAT requires the student to have a thorough understanding of what is tested on the GMAT, how is it tested, and a rigorous preparation.

Taking and Retaking the GMAT

The GMAT can be taken on any working day (subject to availability of slots) and can be scheduled anywhere between 12 months to a day in advance. Registration fee is to $250.

If you have read or heard multiple things about retaking the GMAT, it is because the GMAT has been altering policies quite regularly.
This is the latest information valid today
The mandatory gap between any two given GMAT attempts is 16 days.
You can repeat the GMAT 5 times in a rolling 365 day period.
The maximum number of attempts you can make on the GMAT is 8.

Score Cancellation.

Again a series of changes have happened in the last few years, so the latest on this is
You can see the score and decide to cancel on the day of the exam (free)or up to 72 hours after the exam (for a fee). Canceled scores are not shown on your score report when you report scores to the schools. You can reinstate the scores anytime in the next 4 years and 11 months (for a fee). The GMAT enhanced score report is available even if you cancel the exam.

Score Validity and reporting.

GMAT scores are valid for 5 years. Most business schools require the GMAT to be valid on the date of commencement rather than the date of application On the day of the exam you can report your scores to 5 universities for free. Additional score report can be availed at a cost of $28 per school after the official score report is available.
